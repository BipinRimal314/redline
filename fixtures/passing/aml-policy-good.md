# Anti-Money Laundering Policy

Version 2.1

## Purpose

This policy establishes the Anti-Money Laundering program for the institution in compliance with the Bank Secrecy Act and FinCEN regulations under 31 CFR 1020.210.

## Scope

This policy applies to all employees, contractors, and agents of the institution.

## Definitions

**BSA/AML** — Bank Secrecy Act and Anti-Money Laundering regulations.

**SAR** — Suspicious Activity Report filed with FinCEN.

**CTR** — Currency Transaction Report required for transactions exceeding $10,000.

**CDD** — Customer Due Diligence procedures.

**EDD** — Enhanced Due Diligence for high-risk customers.

**Beneficial Owner** — Any individual who owns 25% or more of a legal entity customer.

## Policy Statement

The institution maintains a comprehensive BSA/AML compliance program that includes internal controls, independent testing, a designated compliance officer, and employee training.

The BSA Compliance Officer is responsible for day-to-day oversight of the AML program and reports directly to the Board of Directors.

## Procedures

### Customer Identification Program (CIP)

The institution verifies the identity of all customers at account opening using government-issued identification documents.

### Transaction Monitoring

Internal controls include automated transaction monitoring systems that flag transactions exceeding $10,000 for CTR filing. The system also monitors for structuring, where customers break up transactions to evade CTR reporting thresholds.

Multiple transactions by or on behalf of the same customer are aggregated on a cumulative daily basis.

### Suspicious Activity Reporting

When suspicious activity is detected, a SAR is filed with FinCEN. SAR narratives include specific dollar amounts, specific dates, and the five W's: who, what, when, where, and why the activity is suspicious.

### Customer Due Diligence

CDD procedures require identification of beneficial owners of legal entity customers at the 25% ownership threshold. Ongoing monitoring of customer accounts is conducted to detect unusual patterns.

Enhanced due diligence is applied to high-risk customers, including politically exposed persons and customers in high-risk jurisdictions.

### Currency Transaction Reports

A CTR is filed within 15 days for all currency transactions exceeding $10,000. Phase I and Phase II exempt customers are documented per FinCEN guidance.

## Roles and Responsibilities

The BSA Compliance Officer oversees the program. Department managers ensure their staff complete required training. The Board of Directors reviews the program annually.

## Review and Approval

This policy is subject to independent audit annually by a qualified third-party auditor. Results are reported to the Board.

BSA/AML training is required for all employees within 30 days of hire and annually thereafter.
