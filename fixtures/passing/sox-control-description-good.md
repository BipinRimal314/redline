# Revenue Recognition Internal Controls

Version 3.0

## Purpose

This document describes the internal controls over the revenue recognition process for quarterly and annual financial reporting.

## Scope

These controls apply to all revenue transactions processed through the order-to-cash cycle.

## Definitions

**ICFR** — Internal Controls over Financial Reporting.

**Control Owner** — The individual responsible for executing and maintaining the control.

**Key Control** — A control that directly addresses a significant risk to financial reporting.

## Policy Statement

The company maintains internal controls over financial reporting in accordance with SOX Section 404 and PCAOB AS 2201.

## Procedures

### Control 1: Revenue Transaction Authorization

**Control Owner:** Regional Sales Director

**Frequency:** Every transaction

**Description:** All revenue transactions exceeding $50,000 require dual authorization. The sales representative initiates the order, and the Regional Sales Director must approve before the order is processed in the ERP system. Approval is documented via electronic signature with timestamp in the order management module.

**Test Procedure:** Select a random sample of 25 revenue transactions exceeding $50,000 from the quarter. For each transaction, verify: (1) electronic approval from the Regional Sales Director exists, (2) approval timestamp precedes the order processing date, (3) the approver is not the same person who initiated the order. Expected result: 100% compliance rate. Any exception constitutes a control failure.

### Control 2: Month-End Revenue Cutoff

**Control Owner:** Revenue Accounting Manager

**Frequency:** Monthly

**Description:** On the last business day of each month, the Revenue Accounting Manager runs the cutoff report from the ERP system and reconciles all revenue transactions against shipping documentation. Transactions shipped after the cutoff date are deferred to the following period. The reconciliation is reviewed and signed by the Controller within 3 business days.

**Test Procedure:** Obtain the cutoff reconciliation for each month in the quarter. For each reconciliation, verify: (1) the reconciliation was completed on the last business day, (2) all transactions match shipping dates, (3) the Controller's review signature and date are present within 3 business days. Select 10 transactions near the cutoff date and trace to shipping documentation. Expected result: 100% proper period assignment.

### Control 3: Segregation of Duties

The following duties are segregated across different individuals: order entry (Sales), order approval (Sales Director), revenue recording (Revenue Accounting), and cash receipt processing (Treasury). No individual performs more than one of these functions. Access controls in the ERP system enforce this segregation through role-based permissions reviewed quarterly by IT Security.

## Roles and Responsibilities

The Controller oversees the revenue recognition control environment. The Internal Audit team tests controls quarterly. The Audit Committee reviews results annually.

## Review and Approval

These control descriptions are reviewed annually by the Controller and approved by the CFO.
